Color Theory (for actual houses, not art school)

1. Undertones Matter

White paint is not just "white." It can lean warm (yellow/beige), cool (blue/gray), or neutral. Putting a cool white next to warm wood can make the wood look orange.

2. Pick a Dominant Neutral + 1 Accent

Rooms feel calmer if one color is in charge. Example: warm greige walls + black hardware + a repeating sage accent in pillows / art / throw.

3. Mood by Color Family

  • Sage / Olive: grounded, calm, earthy.
  • Terracotta / Clay: warm, lived-in, boho, cozy.
  • Deep Navy: dramatic, tailored, more "finished."
  • Greige / Taupe: safe, flexible, renter-friendly.

4. Cheat Code for Wall Color

Sample the color on poster boards, not directly on the wall. Move the board around the room morning / afternoon / night. If it looks sickly green or purple at night, don't use it.
