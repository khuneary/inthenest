Design Style Guides

Below are approachable snapshots of popular interior design styles. Use them to describe what you like (or don’t like) and to shop without getting pushed into trends that don’t feel like you.

Boho (Bohemian)

Relaxed, layered, collected. Natural fibers (linen, jute, rattan), warm neutrals with pops of coral, terracotta, and sage. Plants everywhere. Imperfect is good.

Modern

Clean lines, smooth surfaces, low visual clutter. Neutral bases like black / white / gray with controlled accent colors. Metal, glass, sleek wood finishes.

Modern Rustic

Mix of clean modern shapes with warm, weathered textures. Think matte black hardware next to reclaimed wood, linen sofas, stone, iron, warm white walls.

Transitional

Blend of traditional and modern. Softer curves, neutral palettes, nothing too “cold modern” and nothing too “grandma traditional.” Good for families who want timeless.

How to Use This

  • Screenshot the style you like most and bring it when furniture shopping.
  • Stick to 2–3 consistent materials (example: oak + black metal + linen).
  • Repeat colors across the room (ex: same sage pillow tone in artwork).
